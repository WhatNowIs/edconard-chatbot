This chart depicts the estimated drug overdose deaths in the U.S. over the preceding 12 months from 2015 to 2023. The data is sourced from the NVSS/CDC and is presented by Financial Times. 

Here are key insights from the chart:

1. **Overall Trend**: There is a significant upward trend in total drug overdose deaths from 2015 through 2022, peaking in 2022 before slightly declining in 2023.

2. **Deaths by Opioid Type**: 
   - **Total**: The total number of drug overdose deaths has seen a marked increase, reaching over 100,000 at its peak.
   - **All opioids** (including synthetic opioids like fentanyl): Deaths involving all opioids are a major component of the total overdose deaths.
   - **Specific trend for Synthetic opioids** (such as fentanyl): There is a notable rise in deaths related to synthetic opioids, indicating their growing impact over time.

3. **Annual Comparisons**: 
   - From 2015 to 2017, the growth in overdose deaths was moderate.
   - Between 2017 and 2019, there was a sharper increase.
   - The most significant rise occurs post-2019, reaching its peak in 2022.
   
4. **Recent Decline**: The chart indicates a slight decline in overdose deaths from the peak in 2022 to early 2023, though the numbers remain high relative to earlier years.

Overall, the chart highlights the escalating crisis of drug overdoses in the U.S., particularly driven by synthetic opioids like fentanyl.
