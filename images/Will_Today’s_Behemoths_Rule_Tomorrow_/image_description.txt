The graph in Figure 3 compares the returns of the "Big 5" stocks, the S&P 500, and Large Cap Value returns from 1995 to 2012.

Key points summarized:

1. **Time Frame**: The data spans from 1995 to 2012.
2. **Metrics Covered**:
   - **Big 5** (solid line)
   - **S&P 500** (dashed line)
   - **Large Cap Value** (dotted line)

3. **Trend Analysis**:
   - All three metrics started at a similar base value in 1995.
   - By the year 2000, the Large Cap Value showed a significant increase, peaking around 350.
   - The S&P 500 and Big 5 also exhibited growth, but to a lesser extent, peaking around 250 and 200, respectively.
   - Following 2000, all metrics experienced a decline with some fluctuation.
   - Large Cap Value demonstrated a higher level of volatility compared to the other two metrics, particularly noticeable around 2006 and 2008.
   - From 2009 onwards, there is a steady upward trend across all three metrics, culminating in another significant spike by 2012.

4. **Summary**: Over the period, Large Cap Value showed greater peaks and valleys, highlighting higher volatility. The S&P 500 maintained more consistent growth but still followed the general trend seen across all three metrics. The Big 5's performance remained relatively more stable compared to Large Cap Value but still exhibited similar overall trends.

Source: Ken French, Capital IQ, Verdad analysis.
