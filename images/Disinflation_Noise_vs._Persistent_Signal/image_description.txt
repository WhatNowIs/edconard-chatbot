### Summary of "Menu Costs" for Full Service Meals and Snacks

The chart and accompanying text provide insights into the price changes of sit-down restaurant meals, a useful indicator of broader inflationary trends.

**Key Points:**

1. **Current Dynamics**: 
   - The pace of price increases for sit-down restaurant meals has significantly slowed since the end of last year.
   - Despite the slowdown, the rate of price increase remains several percentage points higher compared to pre-pandemic levels.

2. **Data Representation**:
   - **Yearly (Blue solid line)**: Demonstrates the annual rate of price changes.
   - **Month-over-Month Annualized, 4-month Moving Average (Red dotted line)**: Shows the shorter-term fluctuations smoothed over a span of four months.

3. **Historical Trends**:
   - From 1999 to 2019, the yearly price changes fluctuated mostly between 2% and 4%.
   - Noticeable dips occurred around 2009 (post the financial crisis) and gradual recovery observed afterward.
   - Sharp increases are observed starting around 2020, likely associated with the economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.

4. **Recent Trends**:
   - Extremely high spikes in prices are noted around 2021 and continued through early 2022.
   - Recent months have shown a decline, yet these rates are higher compared to historical norms before the pandemic.

**Source**:
- The data is sourced from the Bureau of Labor Statistics and includes calculations by Matthew Klein.

This comprehensive overview indicates a historical comparison of price dynamics for sit-down restaurant meals, highlighting significant trends and economic impacts over the past two decades.
