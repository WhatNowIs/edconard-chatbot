The chart titled "Banks' willingness to lend to customers approaching 2008 levels" presents a historical view of banks' willingness to lend to consumers from Q3 1966 to Q4 2022, as measured by the FRB Sr. Officers Survey. 

Key Points:
1. **Volatility and Trend Analysis**: The willingness to lend is highly volatile, with significant peaks and troughs occurring periodically.
2. **Recession Indicators**: Periods shaded in gray indicate recessions, during which lending willingness typically drops sharply.
3. **Recent Trends**: As of Q4 2022, banks' willingness to lend is declining, nearing low levels observed during the 2008 Financial Crisis.
4. **Historical Highs and Lows**:
   - Peaks: Notable spikes in lending willingness can be observed between the late 1960s and early 1970s, early 1980s, and late 1990s.
   - Lows: Significant declines are evident around Q2 1970, early 1980s, early 2000s, and during the 2008 financial crisis.

Source: Data is provided by FRB, Bloomberg, and interpreted by Apollo Chief Economist. This chart underscores the cyclical nature of banking willingness to lend in response to economic conditions and recessions.
