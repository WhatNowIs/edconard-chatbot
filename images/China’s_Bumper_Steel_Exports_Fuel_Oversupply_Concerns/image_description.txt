The chart illustrates China's steel exports in January-February, measured in million tonnes, from 2004 to 2024. Key insights include:

1. A significant rise in steel exports occurred between 2014 and 2016, peaking at about 18 million tonnes.
2. From 2016 to 2019, exports somewhat decreased and stabilized between 10 to 15 million tonnes.
3. There was a noticeable decline in 2020, followed by variable performance in subsequent years.
4. In 2024, steel exports surged dramatically, reaching the highest level in eight years, surpassing 15 million tonnes.

This trend shows a marked recovery and growth in Chinese steel exports over recent years, culminating in a substantial increase in 2024.
