The image is a visual presentation of survey data from the Survey Center on American Life on perceptions of gender equality among young adults (ages 18 to 29) in the U.S. The survey compares responses between 2017 and 2024, focusing on whether young men and women feel the U.S. has "Gone Too Far," "Been About Right," or "Not Gone Far Enough" in promoting gender equality.

### Key Findings:
1. **Young Men:**
   - **Gone Too Far:** Increased from 8% in 2017 to 19% in 2024.
   - **Been About Right:** Decreased from 41% in 2017 to 37% in 2024.
   - **Not Gone Far Enough:** Decreased from 50% in 2017 to 41% in 2024.

2. **Young Women:**
   - **Gone Too Far:** Remained relatively stable, with a slight increase from 8% in 2017 to 9% in 2024.
   - **Been About Right:** Slight increase from 25% in 2017 to 26% in 2024.
   - **Not Gone Far Enough:** Decreased from 66% in 2017 to 60% in 2024.

### Summary:
- There has been a notable increase in the percentage of young men who believe that the U.S. has "Gone Too Far" in promoting gender equality, rising from 8% in 2017 to 19% in 2024.
- Conversely, a declining percentage of young men believe that the U.S. has "Not Gone Far Enough," dropping from 50% to 41% over the same period.
- For young women, perceptions have remained relatively stable with minor changes. A slight increase in the perception that the U.S. has "Gone Too Far" and "Been About Right," while the belief that the U.S. has "Not Gone Far Enough" in promoting gender equality has decreased from 66% to 60%.

The data underscores a divergence in perceptions of gender equality progress between young men and women over the years.
