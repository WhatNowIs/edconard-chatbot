The chart titled "Equities Get Growth Benefit" illustrates the relationship between India's market capitalization and its GDP from 2003 to 2024. The black line represents India's current GDP, while the blue line represents India's market capitalization. 

Key observations from the chart include:

1. Overall Growth: Both India's GDP and market capitalization have shown significant growth from 2003 to 2024.
2. Correlation: India's market capitalization closely tracks its GDP, indicating a strong correlation between the two metrics.
3. Fluctuations: There are noticeable fluctuations in market capitalization, particularly around the global financial crisis in 2008-2009, and again around 2020-2021, coinciding with the COVID-19 pandemic.
4. Recent Surge: Both India's GDP and market capitalization have seen a steep rise from 2022 onwards, with market capitalization nearing $4 trillion by 2024.

The source of data used in the chart is the World Bank and Bloomberg.
