The chart titled "Exhibit 7: Net Income contribution by sector to S&P 500, 1975-2024" provides a visual representation of the evolving contributions of different sectors to the net income of the S&P 500 over nearly five decades, concluding as of March 31, 2024.

### Key Insights:

1. **Historic Sector Dominance:**
   - The Energy sector experienced significant influence in the late 1970s and early 1980s, peaking during the "Energy Bubble" when it contributed 27% to the total net income.
   - The Technology sector saw a substantial spike during the late 1990s to early 2000s, known as the "Tech Bubble," contributing up to 14%.
   - The Financial sector showed a marked increase in contribution leading up to the 2008 financial crisis and peaked during the so-called "Financials Bubble" at 29%.

2. **Current Sector Contributions (as of March 31, 2024):**
   - The Financials sector remains a major contributor at 21%.
   - The Information Technology sector contributes 20%.
   - The Health Care sector and Industrials each contribute 9%.
   - Consumer Discretionary and Consumer Staples sectors provide 9% and 7%, respectively.
   - Communication Services contribute 10%.
   - Relatively smaller contributions come from Real Estate (1%), Utilities (3%), Materials (2%), and Energy (8%).

3. **Sectoral Dynamics:**
   - Over the years, there's a noticeable shift towards greater contributions from Technology and Financial sectors.
   - Energy sector's contribution has reduced significantly from its peaks in the 1970s and early 1980s.
   - Health Care and Consumer sectors show consistent yet moderate contributions throughout the observed period.

### Conclusion:
The chart highlights the shifting economic dynamics as different sectors wax and wane in relevance and profitability. The dominance has shifted from Energy in the 1980s, to Technology in the early 2000s, and to Financials in the late 2000s through the present day. This reflects broader economic and market trends, including technological advancements, financial globalization, and changes in consumer behavior.
