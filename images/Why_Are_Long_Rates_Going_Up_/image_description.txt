The graph titled "Term premium rising" illustrates the movement of the 10-year Treasury term premium from January 2006 to January 2023. The term premium, measured in percentage points, ranges from -2% to 5%.

Key observations include:

1. **Initial Period (2006-2008)**: From January 2006 to late 2008, the term premium fluctuates but remains generally between 0% and 2%.
  
2. **Global Financial Crisis (2008-2009)**: During the financial crisis around late 2008 and early 2009, there is a notable spike, with the term premium reaching above 4%.
   
3. **Post-Crisis to Mid-2010s (2009-2016)**: Following the spike, the term premium experiences a downward trend, reaching levels below 1% and approaching 0% by 2016.
   
4. **Late 2010s to Pre-Pandemic (2016-2019)**: From 2016 onwards, the term premium continues to decline and dips into negative territory, hovering around -1% by late 2019.
   
5. **COVID-19 Pandemic (2020)**: During the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic in early 2020, there is another marked dip and subsequent fluctuations.
   
6. **Recent Trends (2021-2023)**: In the period from 2021 to early 2023, the term premium shows signs of recovery, moving back towards 0% and even reaching slightly positive values.

Overall, the 10-year Treasury term premium has exhibited significant volatility, influenced heavily by major economic events such as the 2008 financial crisis and the COVID-19 pandemic. The recent trend indicates a rising term premium, moving from negative territory back towards positive values as of January 2023.
