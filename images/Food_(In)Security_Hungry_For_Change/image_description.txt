The graph titled "Exhibit 1: Moderate to severe food insecurity by region (shown in % of population)" illustrates the percentages of the population experiencing food insecurity across different regions for the years 2016, 2019, and 2022. Here are the key takeaways:

1. **Global Overview:**
   - There has been a noticeable increase in food insecurity worldwide from 2016 to 2022.
   - In 2016, 14.8% faced moderate insecurity and 7.9% faced severe insecurity.
   - By 2022, 18.3% faced moderate insecurity and 11.3% faced severe insecurity.

2. **Africa:**
   - Africa experienced the highest food insecurity rates.
   - Severe insecurity jumped from 19.2% in 2016 to 24.0% in 2022.
   - Moderate insecurity also increased from 30.5% to 36.9% in the same period.

3. **Asia:**
   - Both moderate and severe food insecurity levels have risen.
   - Severe insecurity grew from 6.4% in 2016 to 14.6% in 2022.
   - Moderate insecurity climbed from 11.4% to 22.2%.

4. **Latin America (LatAm):**
   - There was a significant increase, especially in severe food insecurity.
   - Severe insecurity more than doubled from 8.8% in 2019 to 12.6% in 2022.
   - Moderate insecurity remained relatively stable, with a minor decrease from 22.2% in 2016 to 21.8% in 2022.

5. **North America/Europe:**
   - Although food insecurity is generally lower in this region, it has seen a slight increase.
   - Severe insecurity rose from 0.9% in 2019 to 1.5% in 2022.
   - Moderate insecurity increased from 6.2% to 6.6% in the same period.

In conclusion, food insecurity has worsened in most regions since 2016 and especially since 2019, highlighting significant disparities, with Africa and Asia experiencing the highest increases.
