The graph titled "Getting Higher" illustrates the positivity rates for marijuana usage across various industries from 2018 to 2022, as per data sourced from Quest Diagnostics. 

Key points include:

1. **Industries Covered**:
   - Accommodation and Food Services (Dark Red Line)
   - Retail Trade (Light Red Line)
   - Transportation and Warehousing (Peach Line)
   - Real Estate Rental and Leasing (Olive Line)
   - Wholesale Trade (Beige Line)

2. **Trends Over Time**:
   - There is a general upward trend in marijuana positivity rates across all these industries over the observed period.
   - **Accommodation and Food Services**: Started from around 5% in 2018 and increased steadily to reach approximately 8% in 2022.
   - **Retail Trade**: Increased from approximately 4% in 2018 to about 7% in 2022.
   - **Transportation and Warehousing**: Started around 3.5% in 2018 and rose to about 6% by 2022.
   - **Real Estate Rental and Leasing**: This rate increased from around 3% in 2018 to near 4.5% in 2022.
   - **Wholesale Trade**: This rate began at approximately 2.5% in 2018 and climbed to nearly 4% by 2022.

Overall Summary:
Marijuana positivity rates have significantly increased across various industries from 2018 to 2022, with Accommodation and Food Services and Retail Trade industries witnessing the highest increases. This suggests a notable rise in marijuana usage among employees within these sectors over the past five years.
