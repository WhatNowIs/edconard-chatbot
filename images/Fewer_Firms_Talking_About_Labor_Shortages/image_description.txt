The chart illustrates mentions of "labor shortage(s)" on corporate calls from Q1 2020 to Q1 2023. It shows a significant increase in mentions during the early stages of the COVID-19 pandemic, peaking in Q4 2021. Although the frequency has decreased since then, discussions about labor shortages continue to occur at higher levels compared to pre-pandemic periods.

Key Points:
1. Mentions were minimal in early 2020.
2. A sharp rise started in Q2 2021, peaking in Q4 2021.
3. Although mentions declined after Q4 2021, they remain higher than pre-pandemic levels.
4. The data sources include earnings calls, conference calls, shareholder management calls, guidance calls, and M&A and sales results calls.

Source: Bloomberg/Michael McDonough, Apollo Chief Economist.
