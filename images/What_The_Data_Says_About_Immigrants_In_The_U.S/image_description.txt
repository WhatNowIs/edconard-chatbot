The graph from Pew Research Center details the immigrant share of the U.S. population from 1850 to 2022. Here are the key points:

1. **Historical Trends**:
   - In 1850, immigrants constituted 9.7% of the U.S. population, with 2.24 million immigrants out of a total population of 23.19 million.
   - Immigrant share peaked in 1890 at 14.8%.
   - From the 1900s through 1970, there was a steady decline in the immigrant share, reaching a low of 4.7% in 1970.

2. **Modern Trends**:
   - Post-1970, there has been a consistent increase in the immigrant share of the population.
   - By 2022, immigrants made up 13.8% of the U.S. population, with 46.12 million immigrants out of a total population of 333.29 million.

3. **Demographic Shifts**:
   - Approximately half of all immigrants from 1965 to 2022 originated from Latin America.

This detailed overview highlights significant shifts in the immigrant composition of the U.S., underscoring notable historical peaks and modern demographic changes.
